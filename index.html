Privacy Policy

Rabbit Hole (com.jacopo.rabbithole) · Effective date: July 11, 2026

Summary: Rabbit Hole has no accounts and no servers of its own. Your game progress stays on your device. The only personal data processing happens through Google AdMob, which serves the ads that keep the app free, and through Wikipedia, which supplies the articles you explore.

Who we are

Rabbit Hole is developed by Jacopo ("we", "us"). For any privacy question or request, contact us at rabbithole.wikipediagame@gmail.com.

Data stored on your device only

The following is saved locally on your device and never uploaded by us:

  • Game statistics, personal bests, daily-challenge streaks and achievements
  • App settings (e.g. notification preference)
  • Topic keywords derived from articles you engage with, used to personalize the Explore feed on-device
  • Cached article images (standard temporary cache)

Deleting the app deletes all of this data. There is no account to delete because none is ever created.

Wikipedia content

Articles, images and links are fetched live from the Wikimedia (Wikipedia) API. When the app requests an article, Wikimedia's servers receive your IP address and a standard technical request header, as with any website visit. Their handling of that data is described in the Wikimedia Privacy Policy. We do not send Wikimedia anything about you beyond those requests.

Advertising (Google AdMob)

The app shows ads served by Google AdMob: native ads in the Explore feed and after games, and optional rewarded ads you can choose to watch in exchange for a hint. To serve and measure ads, Google may collect:

  • Your device's advertising identifier (Android Advertising ID / Apple IDFA, where permitted)
  • IP address and coarse location derived from it
  • Device and app information, and ad interaction data (views, clicks)

How Google uses this data is described in Google's Privacy Policy and How Google uses information from AdMob.

  • European Economic Area / UK: on first launch you are shown a consent dialog (Google's UMP framework). If you decline, ads are non-personalized. You can change your choice from the app's settings.
  • iOS: the system App Tracking Transparency prompt asks your permission before the advertising identifier is used for tracking. If you decline, ads are not personalized using it.
  • Opt-out anytime: you can reset or delete your advertising ID in your device settings (Android: Settings → Privacy → Ads · iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking).

Notifications

If you enable the daily reminder, a notification is scheduled locally on your device. No push-notification service is used and nothing is sent to us. You can turn it off in the app's settings or your system settings at any time.

What we don't do

  • We do not require or create accounts
  • We do not collect names, emails, contacts, photos, precise location or messages
  • We do not sell personal data
  • We do not operate our own analytics or tracking beyond what is described above

Children

Rabbit Hole is a general-audience app and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. In regions with ad consent requirements, ads are served non-personalized unless consent is given.

Your rights

Depending on where you live (e.g. GDPR in the EU/UK, CCPA in California), you may have rights to access, correct or delete personal data. Because we hold no personal data ourselves, such requests generally concern Google's ad systems — see the links above — but you can always contact us at rabbithole.wikipediagame@gmail.com and we will help.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, the new version will be published at this address with an updated effective date.
